Antes de la Forma: The Threshold Before Image

A curatorial reflection on perception, silence and the moment before an image becomes recognizable.

Article Details

Category Journal

Topic Current Exhibition

Location San Miguel de Allende

Reading Time 4 min

Before an image becomes a recognizable figure, there is a moment of suspension. A threshold where form has not yet settled and perception remains open. Antes de la Forma begins precisely in that unstable interval: the instant before certainty, before narrative, before the eye decides what it is seeing.

The exhibition gathers works that appear to resist immediate definition. Surfaces, textures and gestures operate as fragments of a visual language that is still forming. Rather than presenting the artwork as a fixed answer, the exhibition proposes an experience of looking as a slow act of construction.

“The work does not ask to be solved. It asks to be approached, observed and inhabited.”

At ON! Gallery, this exhibition becomes a meditation on how contemporary art creates meaning through ambiguity. The pieces invite the viewer to remain inside the question, to engage with material presence before translating it into symbol or image.

In the context of San Miguel de Allende, where history, architecture and contemporary practice constantly intersect, Antes de la Forma positions the gallery as a space for critical intimacy. Each work opens a dialogue between memory and perception, between what is visible and what has not yet arrived.

The exhibition is also an invitation to collectors to think beyond acquisition as possession. To collect contemporary art is to preserve a moment of inquiry, to support an artist’s evolving language and to participate in the cultural conversations that shape the present.
